📚 Key Definitions

Gender

A set of social and behavioral norms that within a specific culture are widely considered socially appropriate for either male or female. It refers to characteristics that distinguish between males and females in society.

Gender Roles

A set of responsibilities assigned to either males or females according to a particular society. These roles define what tasks and duties are expected from each gender.

Barriers Women Face in Agriculture

Land Ownership

Under customary laws, women do not own land. Land is predominantly owned by men.

Land Use Decisions

Most decisions on land use are made by men without consulting women.

Access to Credit

Women are often denied credit by husbands and cannot use customary land as loan security.

Extension Services

More men than women access extension and training services in agriculture.

Farm Income Control

Men make decisions on selling produce and using family finances without consultation.

Traditional Views

Women are regarded as weak and cannot own property independently.

Principles of Equal Division of Labour

🤝

Teamwork

Men and women should work together as a unified team in all agricultural activities.

🔄

Cooperation

Both genders should cooperate in all farming activities for maximum productivity.

⚖️

Fair Distribution

Work on the farm should be distributed fairly based on ability, not gender.

💬

Consultation

Both men and women should consult each other on farm decisions and activities.

Benefits of Gender Balance in Agriculture

🤝

Genuine Cooperation

Brings about authentic cooperation and harmony within the family unit.

🏆

Ownership Feeling

Promotes positive feelings of ownership in farm enterprises for both partners.

🎯

Shared Vision

Creates shared vision and responsibility as both participate in production.

📝 Key Takeaways

Gender roles in agriculture have traditionally been divided, with women performing the majority of farm work while having limited control over resources and decision-making.

Gender imbalances create significant negative impacts on agricultural productivity, food security, and household income.

Equal participation and gender balance in agriculture leads to better cooperation, shared ownership, increased productivity, and improved family wellbeing.

Both men and women must work together in decision-making for sustainable and successful agricultural production.
