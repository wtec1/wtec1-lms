THEMES IN THE PEARL

MSCE Literature Study Guide – John Steinbeck

Greed and Corruption

  • The pearl buyers try to cheat Kino by offering unfair prices.
  • The doctor refuses to treat Coyotito until he hears of Kino's wealth.
  • The villagers envy Kino and begin to covet the pearl.
  • Kino is attacked by men who want to steal the pearl.
  • The pearl destroys peace and becomes a symbol of evil.

Hope and Ambition

  • Kino dreams of educating Coyotito.
  • The pearl represents a better future and social advancement.
  • Kino believes the pearl will provide status and respect.
  • The family imagines new clothes and a better home.
  • The initial joy and excitement fuel Kino’s ambition.

Family and Protection

  • Kino and Juana work together to save Coyotito after the scorpion sting.
  • Juana risks her life to protect her child and husband.
  • Kino fights attackers to defend his family and the pearl.
  • Juana wants to destroy the pearl because it endangers the family.
  • The family bond becomes the emotional core of the story.

Social Inequality and Oppression

  • The doctor refuses to treat Coyotito because Kino is poor and indigenous.
  • The town authorities exploit the poor through unfair trade.
  • Kino’s poverty makes him powerless in society.
  • The pearl exposes the social divide between rich and poor.

Fate and Free Will

  • The scorpion sting feels like a cruel twist of fate.
  • Kino believes the pearl will allow him to control his destiny.
  • Events spiral beyond Kino’s control despite his efforts.
  • Coyotito’s death suggests destiny cannot be escaped.

Good vs. Evil

  • Kino's peaceful life is threatened by greed and violence.
  • The pearl shifts from a symbol of hope to a symbol of evil.
  • Juana senses the pearl has become evil and wants it gone.
  • Men attempt murder to possess the pearl.

Man vs. Nature

  • The scorpion symbolizes natural danger and fate.
  • Kino relies on the sea for pearls and survival.
  • The sea gives and takes—provides the pearl and destroys Kino’s dreams.

The Illusion of Wealth

  • The pearl appears to promise prosperity and status.
  • Kino believes wealth will solve every problem.
  • The pearl brings suffering instead of happiness.
  • The dream collapses when Coyotito dies.

Sacrifice and Loss

  • Kino loses peace, reputation, and relationships.
  • Kino sacrifices safety for ambition.
  • Coyotito’s death is the ultimate cost of greed.

The Cycle of Life

  • The pearl returns to the sea at the end of the story.
  • Kino rejects materialism and restores natural balance.
  • The ending symbolizes acceptance and renewal.

“In the town they tell the story…” – John Steinbeck
