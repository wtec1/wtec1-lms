CHARACTER TRAITS ANALYSIS

Based on The Pearl by John Steinbeck

Kino

  • Proud – values dignity and status
  • Ambitious – hopes the pearl will improve his life
  • Protective – determined to save Coyotito
  • Obsessive – becomes emotionally attached to the pearl
  • Suspicious – distrusts others as greed rises
  • Violent – resorts to aggression to defend the pearl
  • Tragic – loses his son and dreams due to obsession

Juana

  • Devoted – deeply cares for her family
  • Wise – senses danger in the pearl early
  • Courageous – confronts threats despite fear
  • Traditional – trusts natural remedies
  • Protective – urges Kino to abandon the pearl
  • Resilient – endures hardship and tragedy

Coyotito

  • Innocent – symbolizes purity and hope
  • Vulnerable – his injury drives the story
  • Symbolic – represents the cost of Kino’s ambition

The Doctor

  • Greedy – only helps when wealth is involved
  • Prejudiced – refuses service due to Kino’s class
  • Corrupt – symbolizes colonial inequality

The Priest

  • Traditional – represents religious authority
  • Interested – drawn to the pearl’s value
  • Conflicted – blends faith with material desire

The Pearl Buyers

  • Deceptive – collude to cheat Kino
  • Manipulative – exploit Kino’s hopes
  • Greedy – driven by profit at any cost

The Trackers

  • Ruthless – pursue Kino without mercy
  • Violent – responsible for Coyotito’s death
  • Predatory – symbolize danger and greed

The Neighbor

  • Curious – watches Kino’s fortune unfold
  • Influenced – reflects village gossip and envy

The Canoe Maker

  • Traditional – preserves indigenous heritage
  • Practical – symbolizes cultural continuity

Minor Characters

  • The Beggars – reflect poverty and dependence
  • The Townspeople – show jealousy and shifting loyalties
  • The Neighbor Women – reinforce social gossip
  • Pearl Buyer’s Assistant – represents exploitation
  • Community Leaders – uphold tradition and authority

Symbolic Figures

  • The Scorpion – symbolizes danger and fate
  • The Pearl – symbolizes hope, greed, and destruction

“The pearl had become my soul.” – John Steinbeck
