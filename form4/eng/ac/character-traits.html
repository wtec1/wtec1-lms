CHARACTER TRAITS ANALYSIS

Individual Character Personalities Based on Their Behavior

Macbeth

  • Ambitious - desires power and kingship above all else
  • Courageous - brave warrior in battle initially
  • Weak-willed - easily manipulated by his wife and the witches
  • Guilt-ridden - tormented by conscience after murders
  • Paranoid - constantly fears losing power and being discovered
  • Ruthless - orders multiple murders to secure his throne
  • Imaginative - sees visions and ghosts reflecting his guilt
  • Tyrannical - rules through fear and violence
  • Isolated - becomes increasingly alone as play progresses
  • Indecisive - wavers between action and moral hesitation initially
  • Despairing - loses all meaning and hope by the end

Lady Macbeth

  • Ambitious - ruthlessly pursues power through her husband
  • Manipulative - uses psychological tactics to control Macbeth
  • Determined - unwavering in her goals initially
  • Ruthless - willing to kill without hesitation
  • Strong-willed - appears emotionally stronger than Macbeth at first
  • Cold-hearted - rejects feminine compassion for cruelty
  • Guilt-stricken - eventually destroyed by conscience
  • Fragile - mental state deteriorates under guilt's weight
  • Desperate - tries obsessively to cleanse herself of guilt
  • Isolated - becomes separated from Macbeth emotionally
  • Tragic - dies broken by her own actions

Banquo

  • Noble - maintains moral integrity despite temptation
  • Loyal - remains faithful to Duncan and rightful authority
  • Wise - suspicious of the witches' intentions
  • Cautious - questions prophecies rather than acting on them
  • Courageous - brave warrior and companion to Macbeth
  • Principled - resists ambition that corrupts Macbeth
  • Perceptive - suspects Macbeth of foul play
  • Honorable - values righteousness over personal gain
  • Protective - concerned for his son Fleance's safety

King Duncan

  • Trusting - believes in his subjects' loyalty too readily
  • Kind - generous and appreciative of service
  • Noble - represents legitimate and virtuous kingship
  • Gracious - shows gratitude and honor to his thanes
  • Naive - fails to see treachery in those close to him
  • Virtuous - embodies good governance and rightful rule
  • Generous - rewards loyalty with titles and favor

Macduff

  • Loyal - devoted to Scotland and rightful kingship
  • Courageous - willing to risk everything to oppose tyranny
  • Principled - refuses to attend Macbeth's coronation
  • Vengeful - seeks justice for his murdered family
  • Honorable - passes Malcolm's loyalty test
  • Determined - leads the fight against Macbeth
  • Grief-stricken - devastated by family's slaughter
  • Just - fights for righteousness, not personal gain
  • Strong - maintains resolve despite personal tragedy

Malcolm

  • Cautious - flees Scotland to preserve his life
  • Wise - tests Macduff's loyalty before trusting him
  • Strategic - carefully plans his return to power
  • Righteous - concerned about being a worthy king
  • Diplomatic - secures English support for his cause
  • Patient - waits for the right moment to reclaim throne
  • Just - restores order and rewards loyalty
  • Legitimate - rightful heir to Scottish throne
  • Mature - grows into leadership role throughout play

Lady Macduff

  • Loyal - questions but supports her husband
  • Protective - defends her children fiercely
  • Vulnerable - left defenseless by Macduff's departure
  • Resentful - angry at being abandoned
  • Innocent - victim of Macbeth's tyranny
  • Loving - devoted mother to her children
  • Brave - faces assassins with courage
  • Outspoken - voices her fears and frustrations

Lennox

  • Observant - notices suspicious circumstances around murders
  • Diplomatic - speaks carefully under Macbeth's rule
  • Intelligent - uses irony to express doubts about Macbeth
  • Loyal to Scotland - eventually joins rebellion
  • Cautious - waits before openly opposing Macbeth
  • Perceptive - sees through Macbeth's deceptions

Ross

  • Pragmatic - survives by adapting to circumstances
  • Messenger - frequently delivers news throughout play
  • Politically flexible - serves both Macbeth and rebels
  • Compassionate - shows sympathy when delivering tragic news
  • Cautious - careful about openly opposing power
  • Loyal ultimately - joins Malcolm's forces in the end

Fleance

  • Young - son who must flee danger
  • Innocent - caught in political violence
  • Lucky - escapes assassination attempt
  • Symbolic - represents hope for future

Donalbain

  • Cautious - flees to Ireland for safety
  • Perceptive - recognizes danger immediately
  • Practical - prioritizes survival over appearances
  • Wise - understands he cannot trust anyone

The Three Witches

  • Malevolent - enjoy causing chaos and destruction
  • Prophetic - possess supernatural knowledge
  • Cryptic - speak in riddles and half-truths
  • Manipulative - plant seeds of ambition in Macbeth
  • Supernatural - exist outside natural order
  • Sinister - represent dark forces

Hecate

  • Authoritative - commands the three witches
  • Supernatural - goddess of witchcraft
  • Scheming - plans Macbeth's final deception
  • Powerful - controls darker magical forces

The Porter

  • Humorous - provides comic relief
  • Drunk - intoxicated when on duty
  • Philosophical - jokes about hell and damnation
  • Common - represents ordinary people
  • Ironic - his comedy highlights darker themes

Young Siward

  • Brave - fights courageously against Macbeth
  • Honorable - dies in battle for just cause
  • Young - inexperienced but valiant
  • Noble - worthy son of his father

The Doctor

  • Observant - recognizes Lady Macbeth's spiritual illness
  • Wise - knows her condition is beyond medicine
  • Helpless - cannot cure guilt and conscience
  • Discreet - keeps patient confidences
  • Symbolic - represents limits of earthly cures

King Edward

  • Saintly - possesses healing powers
  • Generous - provides sanctuary and military aid
  • Legitimate - represents rightful Christian kingship
  • Compassionate - helps those fleeing tyranny
  • Powerful - commands English forces

The Old Man

  • Wise - experienced observer of life
  • Traditional - represents old values and natural order
  • Symbolic - voice of common people
  • Observant - witnesses unnatural omens

Angus, Menteith, Caithness

  • Loyal to Scotland - join rebellion against Macbeth
  • Courageous - risk lives opposing tyranny
  • Honorable - support rightful succession
  • United - work together for common good

The Murderers

  • Desperate - accept hired killing for payment
  • Ruthless - kill without moral hesitation
  • Corrupted - represent moral decay under tyranny
  • Brutal - murder women and children
  • Expendable - tools of Macbeth's evil

“Fair is foul, and foul is fair.” – Act 1, Scene 1
